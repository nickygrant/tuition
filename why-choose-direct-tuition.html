What tutoring agencies
don't advertise

The commission model inflates your cost

When you book through an agency, you pay the agency's rate, which includes a markup that goes to the agency, not to your tutor. The same tutor operating directly can charge a lower rate while earning the same (or more) from each session. There is no educational reason to use an agency for a subject-specialist tutor you can reach directly.

"PhD-qualified" at an agency often means "has a PhD in anything"

Large tutoring platforms use PhD-qualified as a selling point, but the PhD may be in history, biology, or a different field entirely. A Cambridge PhD in economics and econometric theory is a specific credential that means something very different for a student studying econometrics. Verify qualifications directly, not through an agency's marketing materials.

Generic lesson plans don't work for technical subjects

Agencies scale by training tutors with lesson plan templates. Econometrics, Further Maths, and Oxbridge preparation cannot be taught from a template, they require understanding what specifically a student is missing, and why. This only happens when a tutor builds genuine familiarity with a student over multiple sessions. That requires continuity. Agency systems are not built for continuity.

DBS checks can be verified, and should be

Dr Grant holds a Full Enhanced DBS Certificate on the Update Service, issued November 2025, independently verifiable on request by parents and institutions. When using an agency, always ask to see the tutor's DBS certificate directly, not just the agency's assurance that one exists.
