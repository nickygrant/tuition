Academic research

Papers and presentations

Research in econometric theory: GMM and GEL inference with singular moment variance, identification-robust inference, weak instruments, and applied microeconometrics.

Featured working paper · CeMMAP CWP05/19 Generalised Anderson-Rubin Statistic Based Inference in the Presence of a Singular Moment Variance Matrix Show abstract & links ▾

Nicky L. Grant (University of St Andrews) & Richard J. Smith (University of Cambridge)

Constructs a confidence region with pointwise asymptotically correct size for a parameter of interest using the generalised Anderson-Rubin (GAR) statistic when the moment variance matrix is singular. The large-sample behaviour of the GAR statistic is analysed via a Laurent series expansion around points of moment variance singularity. Under a condition termed first-order moment singularity, the GAR statistic is shown to have a limiting chi-square distribution on parameter sequences converging to the true value. Violation of this condition renders the GAR statistic unbounded asymptotically. The paper details an appropriate discretisation of the parameter space to implement a feasible GAR-based confidence region with pointwise asymptotically correct size. No regularisation or pre-testing is required. Simulation evidence demonstrates the efficacy of the approach.

Keywords: Laurent series expansion · moment indicator · parameter sequences · singular moment matrix · JEL: C13, C31, C51, C52

Journal article · 2019

How sensitive is the average taxpayer to changes in the tax-price of giving?

Peter G. Backus & Nicky L. Grant

International Tax and Public Finance, Vol. 26(2), pp. 317-356, Springer

Abstract

Provides quantile estimates of the tax-price elasticity of charitable donations, controlling for unobserved heterogeneity. Uses the Correlated Random Effects Quantile estimator to address the puzzle that estimated price elasticities for the average taxpayer exceed those for wealthier itemisers.

CeMMAP working paper · 2018 · CWP23/18

GEL-based inference with unconditional moment inequality restrictions

Nicky L. Grant & Richard J. Smith

Centre for Microdata Methods and Practice, Institute for Fiscal Studies

Abstract

Studies generalised empirical likelihood (GEL) methods for estimation of and inference on partially identified parameters in models specified by unconditional moment inequality constraints. Establishes large-sample equivalence with GMM and proposes simple conservative confidence regions for the identified set. Includes simulation evidence comparing GEL and non-diagonal GMM procedures.

Working paper · 2018

GMM Estimation and Inference with (Asymptotically) Singular Moment Variance

Nicky L. Grant

University of Manchester

Abstract

Studies efficient 2-Step GMM estimation from strongly identified linear moment functions with (asymptotically) singular moment variance at the true parameter. Shows the 2-Step GMM estimator converges at rate √n to a non-standard limit distribution, with rate-n convergence in certain directions when the null space of the moment variance matrix does not lie within that of the outer product of the expected Jacobian. Provides examples of (almost) singular moment variance and discusses implications for inference of common empirical methods used to remove singularities.

Working paper

Comment on Caner (2008): Nearly-Singular Design in GMM and Generalised Empirical Likelihood Estimators

Nicky L. Grant

University of Manchester

Abstract

Identifies and corrects errors in the main results of Caner (2008) on GMM and GEL estimators with nearly-singular moment variance. Shows that mistakes in key lemmas invalidate the proofs of the main theorems. A corrected version of the 2-Step GMM results is provided under minimal modifications to Caner's assumptions. Also shows that the identification setup in Caner (2008) is overly restrictive, ruling out OLS as a special case, and proposes a more general identification assumption.

Book chapter · 2012

Overcoming the Many Weak Instrument Problem Using Normalized Principal Components

Nicky Grant

Essays in Honor of Jerry Hausman, Advances in Econometrics, Vol. 29, pp. 107-47. Eds: Baltagi, Hill, Newey, White. Emerald Publishing.

Abstract

Introduces "normalized principal components" (NPCs) for IV estimation in the presence of many weak instruments. Shows that standard principal component reduction methods produce poor small-sample performance, and demonstrates through simulation that NPC-based selection substantially outperforms the standard approach.
