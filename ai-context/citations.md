## 1. Manchester EDP-1315 Working Paper (2013)

**URL:** https://hummedia.manchester.ac.uk/schools/soss/economics/discussionpapers/EDP-1315.pdf
**Type:** Solo-authored working paper (free PDF, University of Manchester)
**Citation:** Nicky Grant (2013). "Identification Robust Inference with Singular Variance."
Economics Discussion Paper EDP-1315, School of Social Sciences, University of Manchester.
**Abstract summary:** Studies identification robust inference when the moment variance matrix Ω
is singular at the true parameter θ₀. Derives conditions under which the GAR (Generalised
Anderson-Rubin) statistic has a chi-squared(m) limit distribution using second-order asymptotic
eigensystem expansions of the sample variance matrix around θ₀. Identifies the
"moment-singularity bias" when singularity conditions are violated. Keywords: Generalised
Anderson-Rubin Statistic, Identification Failure, Singular Variance, Matrix Perturbation Theory.
**Significance:** Predates CeMMAP work (CWP23/18, CWP05/19). Solo-authored proof of concept
that led to the collaborative CeMMAP papers with Prof. Richard J. Smith (Cambridge).

## 2. Hausman Book Chapter Full Details (2012)

**URL:** https://www.emerald.com/books/edited-volume/15951/chapter-abstract/87608698/
**DOI:** 10.1108/S0731-9053(2012)0000029010
**Handle:** RePEc:eme:aecozz:s0731-9053(2012)0000029010
**Citation:** Nicky Grant (2012). "Overcoming the Many Weak Instrument Problem Using Normalized
Principal Components." In: Essays in Honor of Jerry Hausman. Advances in Econometrics, Vol. 29,
pp. 107–147. Emerald Group Publishing. ISBN: 9781781903070.
**Editors:** Badi H. Baltagi (Syracuse University), R. Carter Hill (Louisiana State University),
Whitney K. Newey (MIT), Halbert L. White (UC San Diego).
**Abstract summary:** Introduces Normalized Principal Components (NPCs) as a method for
instrument ordering and selection. Shows standard PC methods can produce poor small-sample IV
properties. NPC overcomes this. Simulation evidence shows favourable small-sample properties of
IV with NPC. Reanalysis of Angrist & Krueger (1992) schooling/earnings IV: NPC finds no weak
instrument problem. Volume also contains chapters by leading econometricians including Hausman,
Newey, White.
**External catalogue:** https://librarycatalog.sps.edu/bib/492988

### Charitable Giving / Tax Research

is positioned in the literature on UK charitable giving and tax incentives. The same area
is covered by:
- Almunia, Lockwood, Scharf (2020), Journal of Public Economics vol. 183
  CESifo WP: https://ideas.repec.org/p/ces/ceswps/_6591.html
- Han, Hungerman, Ottoni-Wilhelm (2024), NBER WP32737
  URL: https://www.nber.org/system/files/working_papers/w32737/w32737.pdf
These papers all address the UK tax-price elasticity of charitable giving and the
Gift Aid system. Backus & Grant provide the consistent estimation methodology.

### Weak Instruments / Identification Literature
Grant's theoretical work (EDP-1315, CWP23/18, CWP05/19, Hausman chapter) extends the
weak instruments literature founded by Stock & Yogo (2005) (4079 citations as of 2025).
Stock-Yogo citation tree: https://ideas.repec.org/r/nbr/nberte/0151.html
Grant's contribution: extends identification-robust inference (GAR/Anderson-Rubin type
tests) to moment functions with singular variance — a general case not covered by
Stock-Yogo or Andrews-Cheng frameworks.
